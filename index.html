Frequently Asked Questions

Mechanical watches are prized for their craftsmanship and precision engineering. Unlike quartz watches, which are battery-powered, mechanical watches are powered by intricate movements of gears and springs. This traditional watchmaking technique requires exceptional skill to assemble and fine-tune, making each mechanical watch a masterpiece of art and technology. Collectors and enthusiasts often appreciate these watches for their complexity, aesthetic appeal, and the craftsmanship involved in their creation.

Caring for your luxury watch involves regular maintenance and careful handling. It's recommended to have your watch serviced every 3-5 years by a certified professional to ensure its mechanism remains accurate and functional. Keep your watch away from extreme temperatures and magnetic fields, which can affect its performance. For cleaning, lightly wipe it with a soft, dry cloth. If your watch is water-resistant, ensure the crown is fully secured before exposing it to water. Lastly, store your watch in a dry, cool place, preferably in a watch box to protect it from dust and scratches.

Whether you can wear your watch while swimming or diving largely depends on its water resistance rating. Watches are typically rated by their water resistance depth (e.g., 30 meters, 50 meters, 100 meters). A watch with a water resistance of at least 50 meters is suitable for swimming, though for diving, you would want a watch rated at 200 meters or more. Always check your watch's specifications before exposing it to water. Remember, water resistance is not a permanent condition and can be affected by aging of gaskets or accidental impacts; it's advisable to have the water resistance checked annually if you frequently expose your watch to water.
